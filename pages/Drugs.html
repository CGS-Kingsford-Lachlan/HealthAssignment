Drugged and driving

Rules about drugs

Illegal Drugs

The rules about Illegal drugs apply the same way if you are driving a car or not. Although different drugs have their diverse effects you will still recieve the same penalty as each other receives.

Perscription Drugs

Driving a vehicle safely requires good alertness and the ability to act to certain situations with speed. Many prescription medicines when mixed with either alchohol or other drugs can often affect your ability to drive safely.

Combining different medications may have an even greater effect on your ability to concentrate whilst driving. Negative effects of prescription and over-the-counter medications include drowsiness, blurred vision, poor concentration and slower reaction times. Many people who are fined for having drugs in their system are often only using perscription drugs, so its best to read the description on the medication before thinking of driving.

MDT

MDT or mobile drug testing is a key part of catching people under the influence of drugs, although it is primarily used in NSW it is still usefull to learn about it. The Centre for road saftey talks more about it.
